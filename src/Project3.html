User Research Design Project

Project Overview

Introducing the innovative mindfulness app, Clear, designed to support individuals with anxiety and ADHD by promoting mindful living and productivity. This app was designed with the question in mind, Can we create mindful designs with smartphones and apps to better help users with anxiety? Featuring meditation sessions, gentle reminders for essential self-care tasks like hydration and stretching, as well as breathing exercises, journaling prompts, and a dedicated focus mode to minimize phone distractions, the Clear app empowers users to cultivate a balanced and focused lifestyle while reducing procrastination tendencies.

Design brief

The user research process commenced with a survey over two to four weeks, aiming to understand how different types of social media use impact daily mood. This was followed by focus groups within the same timeframe, focusing on mitigating smartphone-induced overwhelm by addressing factors such as constant messages and FOMO triggers. Usability testing, lasted one to three weeks, and assessed the app’s potential to identify users’ anxious or depressive episodes linked to screen time or stressful phone use. Lastly, usage data and customer feedback was analyzed for one to two weeks post-launch, seeking opportunities to enhance the Clear app in aiding users with anxiety and stress related to smartphone use.
